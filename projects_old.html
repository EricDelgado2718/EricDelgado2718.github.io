Hands-On Experience

Autonomous Racecar

Parking Controller Parks at a Specified Distance from the Cone

Real-Time Localization given LIDAR Scan and Map

Given a racecar robot with an NVIDIA Jetson Xavier computer, LIDAR Scanner, ZED Camera, and more advanced technologies, my team and I have been tasked with programming and executing various operations. Our tasks encompass developing and implementing sophisticated functionalities such as wall-following, a safety controller, cone detection, parking control, line following, localization, path planning, and path following. To ensure transparency and collaboration, we conduct bi-weekly Lab Briefings and Reports, during which we discuss the rationale behind our decisions, coding strategies, and collaborative efforts. This project has been a valuable experience, enriching my skills in both technical aspects and in terms of communication and teamwork.

Bionic Prosthetics

Disassembled Bionic Hand

Bionic Hand on Stand

Custom PCB for Dual CAN Interface.

Bionic Prosthetics have, for a long time, been of interest to me. I have had the privilege of working in several bionic prosthetics laboratories and was provided with the resources to construct a bionic hand in Germany. This project utilized a tendon-driven system powered by 16 motors, allowing me to control the four fingers through Arduino units communicating via CAN Bus. I am currently researching in the Biomechatronics lab at MIT, where I am responsible for designing the control system for an ankle-foot prosthetic. This role focuses on optimizing motor activation given individual gait patterns to reduce metabolic expenditure. Through this work, I have gained extensive insights into the capabilities and limitations of both human and mechanical systems, finding the experience profoundly rewarding.
