Hi! I'm Eric Delgado, an MIT '26 from Milwaukee, Wisconsin studying Mechanical Engineering and Robotics

  I currently work in Biomechatronics Group in the Media Lab and am developing a bionic ankle prosthetic with optimal control for biological gait. I also spend a lot of my time as a lead on the MIT Solar Electric Vehicle Team, designing the front suspension. I also like to box and have recently started gymnastics!

Student Researcher, MIT Media Lab - Biomechatronics Lab

  • Develop a humanoid walking plant model in Drake for integration with a Model Predictive Controller, optimizing the gait of an ankle-foot prosthetic based on specified mass and walking speed

  • Use a closed form representation of human walking gait at different speeds to create a library of optimized trajectories to follow given an estimated state

  • Assist supervisor in conducting walking trials with human subjects to validate results

    • Summer Researcher, Friedrich Alexander University - N2 Lab

  • Developed an actuation system with a 33% reduced number of motors while preserving all degrees of freedom for four fingers of a myoelectrically controlled bionic hand prosthetic

  • Coded and implemented a CAN bus communication system facilitated by Arduino nodes to simultaneously and independently actuate tendons of a hand prosthetic

  • Designed and fabricated a damping mechanism meant to simulate muscular stretch during flexion and extension of digits

    • MIT Solar Electric Vehicle Team Lead

  • Design and fabricate a solar powered vehicle that competes in multiple national competitions (American Solar Challenge, Formula Sun Grand Prix)

  • Lead weekly meetings to discuss design choices regarding the suspension system and its implications on the overall design of the solar car

  • Develop and refine the suspension subsystem as the suspension lead by CAD-ing, creating HAAS toolpaths, water jetting, and welding components for final vehicle assembly
